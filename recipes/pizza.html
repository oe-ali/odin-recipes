Pizza


Description

Pizza is a dish that uses kneaded dough that is then shaped into a flat bread. Afterwards a tomato sauce
is spread over the bread, then come the various toppings which vary between mushrooms, onion slices, pepperoni
slices, grated cheese, mozzarella, etc, and in the end the pizza is being baked in the oven.

The origins of this dish trace back to Italy which got popularized thanks to American cooks.


Ingredients for the dough

  • 1 package of dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • half a teaspoon of sugar
  • half a teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of warm water + 1 1/2 cups of warm water
  • 4 3/4 cups of flour + half a cup of flour

Ingredients for the sauce

  • 1 can of tomatoes (about 400-500g)
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano

Steps

  1. Bring out the yeast

    • Dissolve the yeast and sugar in half a cup of warm water.
    • Let it stand for about 5 minutes.

  2. Add more things

    • Now add the 4 3/4 cups of flour, oil, salt and the 1 1/2 cups of water into the yeast mixture.
    • Beat at medium speed for about five minutes.
    • Add a little at a time of the flour that's leftover during the five minutes to keep the dough from being too sticky.

  3. Toss and turn

    • Coat a bowl with 1 tbsp of olive oil.
    • Place the dough in the bowl and turn over to coat all sides.

  4. Take cover!

    • Cover the bowl and then place in an oven that just has the oven light turned on for 45 minutes.

  5. Punch and form!

    • Remove dough from the oven.
    • Punch and turn the dough in the bowl.
    • Repeat this process a few times.
    • Then cut the dough in equal bits until they are roughly the size of a baseball.
    • Shape the dough bits into balls.
    • Cover the dough balls again and place them for 30 minutes into the fridge.

  6. Prepare the sauce

    • Take all the ingredients for the sauce and mix them in a mixer.
    • You now have pizza sauce.

  7. Form the pizza

    • Sprinkle some flour on the table so the dough doesn't stick to it.
    • To form the pizza place your fingers on both hands together.
    • Turn the dough and repeat again and again until you have the crust all around the dough.
    • Next, turn over the dough and using your fingers, smooth over where your indentions from the other side formed,
      going around to complete the full circle.
    • Take the dough into one and and then toss it into the other hand, while you are pulling at the edge of the dough
      to stretch it with the first hand.
    • Now, you are going to take the dough that's in your second hand and toss it to the first hand while pulling at the
      edge of the dough with the second hand.
    • Continue to do this, slightly turning your dough each time and pulling on the bottom part to stretch it out.
    • If you tear the dough, don't worry, just pinch the hole closed when you have it laid out on the pan.
    • The more practice you do, the easier it will be and you won't tear the dough.
    • Alternatively, after forming the crust you could just use both hands to stretch the dough out while it's
      laying on the surface, just stretch the dough, spin a little, stretch, spin, stretch, etc, until you have stretched it all over.

  8. Bake and top it!

    • Bake in an oven that's been preheated to about 250°C for 5 minutes.
    • Take the dough from the oven and start applying sauce the sauce until you reach the crusts of the pizza.
    • Cover with whatever cheese and toppings you want, evenly covering the pizza.
    • Place the pizza back into the oven and turn the heat up to 250°C again.
    • After 8 minutes or when the crust turns golden take the pizza out of the oven.
    • While it's the other pizza's turn you can now slice the finished pizza into slices.
    • Enjoy!
