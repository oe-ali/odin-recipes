Spaghetti


Description

Spaghetti is one of the simplest Italian dishes there is. It is a long, cylindrical pasta that softens when
it's in contact with hot water. When the spaghetti is done it can be eaten as is.

There is a way to give this dish a taste if it isn't enjoyable. Simply creating a tomato sauce with vegetables
and ground beef for instance is enough to raise the taste of the dish.


Ingredients

  • spaghetti pasta from your local convenience store
  • a bit of salt
  • oil or butter
  • tomato sauce
  • grated cheese (optional)
  • ground beef
  • vegetables such as tomatoes, zucchini, mushrooms, etc... (your choice)

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta

    • Bring out the pot of choice.
    • Pour in about 50 percent of water and let it really boil.
    • Add a little bit of salt.
    • Now add the pasta and stir it.
    • As the pasta is starting soften check out its texture.
    • Once the pasta is done discard the water and the noodles into a sieve.
    • Add some oil or butter into the pot that is in use.
    • Throw the pasta back into the pot and mix the contents with low heat.

  2. Prepare the sauce

    • Add some oil or butter into a pan of choice.
    • Once it's sizzling add the ground beef into the pan.
    • While the ground beef is cooking chop up your vegetables. I suggest using tomatoes and onions.
    • Add the diced up vegetables and cook until you notice the onions becoming golden brown.
    • The final step is to include the tomato sauce. Stir the pan until you notice the sauce bubbling then it is done.

  3. Serve the spaghetti

    • With the pasta being on your plate add some of your pasta sauce on top.
    • Additionally you can sprinkle grated cheese on the sauce making it melt slowly.
    • Enjoy your dish!
