Turkish Pide Bread


Description

Pide is a type of flat bread that carries its origins in Turkey. Essentially, this is
a staple of the Turkish breakfast cuisine. This bread is topped with sesame seeds.

There's also a version of pide that looks like a boat which is semi-open so one can see which filling
has been used (either white-cheese-filling, ground beef or mashed potatoes). We will focus on the plain
Turkish Flat Bread, though.


Ingredients

  • Warm milk (wholemilk or 2%)
  • Instant dry yeast
  • Eggs
  • All purpose flour (4 1/2 cups, not more)
  • Yogurt

Steps

  1. Make the dough

    • In a large bowl whisk milk, water, olive oil, sugar, salt, instant dry yeast and egg white.
    • Add the flour one cup at a time and mix well until combined and the dough comes together.
    • Cover the bowl and let it sit for 30 minutes.

  2. Shape the dough

    • Sprinkle the surface generously with flour.
    • Transfer the dough to the surface.
    • Shape it into a boule and make sure it's floured on all sides.
    • Cut the dough into 8 pieces.
    • Place two of the dough balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
    • Cover gently and let them rise for 20 minutes.

  3. Prepare the glaze and final shaping

    • Preheat the oven to 400ÂºF and place an empty baking dish in the lower rack.
    • In a small bowl mix egg yolk, yogurt and water.
    • Brush a bit of the glaze on the dough balls and with your fingers, spread them into round circles with about 1/2 inch thickness.
    • Using your fingers, first make indentations on the dough about 1 inch from the edge to make a circle.
    • Then with your fingers make diagonal indentations in two different directions to make diamonds inside that circle.
    • Repeat the same process with the remaining 6 dough balls.

  4. Bake the bread

    • Once the oven is hot make the indentations again on the dough.
    • Top with sesame seeds and place the baking sheet on the middle rack.
    • Fill the baking dish on the lower rack with 2 cups of water and close the oven door.
    • Bake in the oven for 15-18 minutes until golden on top.
    • Once out of the oven, cover the loaves with a damp paper towel.
    • The steam of the bread with be trapped and your bread will be soft and fluffy.
    • Repeat the baking process with the remaining loaves.
